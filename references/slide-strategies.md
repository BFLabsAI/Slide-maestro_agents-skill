# Slide Strategies — Narrative Arc and Pacing

## Opening (Slides 1-3)
Goal: Hook attention. Set context. Create curiosity.

Techniques:
- Bold statistic that challenges assumptions
- Provocative question
- Striking visual with minimal text
- Short story or anecdote
- "Imagine if..." scenario

Avoid: agenda slides, company logo overload, "About us" openings.

## Middle (Slides 4-N)
Goal: Build argument. Provide evidence. Maintain engagement.

Techniques:
- Alternate between data, narrative, and visual slides
- Vary layout every 2-3 slides
- Use progressive disclosure (reveal information step by step)
- Include a surprise or unexpected insight midway
- Use section dividers to create chapters

Pacing rhythm:
- Data slide → Story slide → Visual slide → Repeat
- Never put 3+ data-heavy slides in a row
- Insert a breathing slide (quote, image, divider) after dense content

## Closing (Slides N-2 to End)
Goal: Drive action. Leave a lasting impression.

Techniques:
- Summarize 3 key takeaways (not more)
- Clear, specific CTA (not "Questions?")
- Memorable final image or statement
- Contact information (but not as the last slide — end with the message)
- Leave the audience with a feeling, not just information

Avoid:
- "Thank you" as the final slide (waste of attention)
- Q&A slide with no content (use a strong closing statement instead)
- New information in the closing

## Slide Transitions
- Section dividers create natural breaks
- Numbering slides gives orientation
- Consistent visual language creates flow
- Return to a motif from the opening for closure

## Speaker Notes Strategy
- Keep notes as talking points, not full scripts
- Include data sources and specific numbers
- Note which slides to skip if running short
- Mark backup slides for anticipated questions
