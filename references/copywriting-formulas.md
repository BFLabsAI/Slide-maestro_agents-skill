# Copywriting Formulas for Slide Presentations

## PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution
Best for: pain-point decks, sales presentations, change management

1. **Problem**: State the pain clearly. "Your onboarding takes 14 days."
2. **Agitation**: Make it hurt. "Every day of delay costs $X in churn."
3. **Solution**: Present your answer. "Our tool cuts it to 2 days."

Slide mapping:
- Slides 1-2: Problem statement (data-backed)
- Slides 3-4: Agitation (consequences, cost of inaction)
- Slides 5+: Solution (features, proof, CTA)

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Best for: pitch decks, product launches, investor presentations

1. **Attention**: Bold opening. Surprising stat, provocative question, striking visual.
2. **Interest**: Context and relevance. Why should they care?
3. **Desire**: Benefits, proof, social evidence. Make them want it.
4. **Action**: Clear CTA. What next step do you want?

Slide mapping:
- Slide 1: Attention (hero stat or question)
- Slides 2-4: Interest (market, problem, opportunity)
- Slides 5-8: Desire (solution, traction, testimonials)
- Slides 9-10: Action (ask, terms, contact)

## SCQA — Situation, Complication, Question, Answer
Best for: strategic decks, board presentations, consulting-style decks

1. **Situation**: The current state. "We are in market X with Y share."
2. **Complication**: What changed or is blocking. "Competitor Z launched at 30% lower price."
3. **Question**: The natural question. "How do we maintain margin?"
4. **Answer**: Your recommendation. "Three strategic options..."

Slide mapping:
- Slide 1: Situation (context setting)
- Slide 2: Complication (the wrinkle)
- Slide 3: Question (the bridge)
- Slides 4+: Answer (recommendations, options, roadmap)

## Star, Chain, Hook
Best for: narrative-driven decks, storytelling, brand presentations

1. **Star**: The main character or hero element
2. **Chain**: A series of connected facts, proof points, or story beats
3. **Hook**: The compelling conclusion or call to action

## Content Writing Rules for Slides

- One idea per slide
- Headlines should be complete thoughts, not labels ("Revenue grew 40%" not "Revenue")
- Bullets: start with verbs, keep under 12 words
- Numbers: use specific figures ("3.2M users" not "millions of users")
- Questions engage more than statements
- Contrast creates interest ("Before vs After", "Old way vs New way")
- End with action, not a "Thank you" slide
